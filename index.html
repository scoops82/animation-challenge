Women in Science

Here are some of the amazing contributions made by women in the history of science. Although due to time constraints only one is introduced below.

Marie Curie

Marie grew up in a poor family in Poland. Her natural curiosity meant that she was drawn to science. Unfortunately, she could not follow her brothers to university in Poland because Polish universities were male only.

She went to Paris to study and became fascinated by x-rays and radiation. In her research she discovered two new elements: Radium and Polonium.

Marie was awarded the Nobel Prize in 1903 and 1911. She remains the only woman to have won the prize twice.
